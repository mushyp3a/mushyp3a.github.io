Complex Conjugate

0001-01-01 Â· [Mackenzie Trafford]

Definition

The Complex Conjugate of a complex number \(Z=x+iy\) is given by:

\[\overline{Z}=\overline{x+iy}=x-iy\]

This operation flips the sign of the imaginary part

Properties

  • When multiplied by the original, the result is always a real number

\[Z \cdot \overline{Z} = (x+iy)*x-iy) = x^2 + y^2\]

Applications

  • Rationalise complex fractions - Operations on Complex Numbers
  • Find the modulus of a complex number
